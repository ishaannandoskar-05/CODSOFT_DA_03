Retail Sales — Data Visualization Report¶

Task 3: Meaningful Visualizations with Matplotlib, Seaborn & Plotly

Dataset: synthetic Superstore-style retail sales data (2,200 orders, Jan 2023 – Dec 2024) covering Category, Sub-Category, Region, Segment, Sales, Profit, Quantity and Discount.

This notebook builds a bar chart, line chart, pie chart, histogram, and scatter plot — each customized with titles, axis labels, legends, and a consistent color scheme — followed by a short written insight per chart, and a bonus interactive Plotly dashboard.

2. Bar Chart — Total Sales & Profit by Category¶

Compares revenue against profit across the three product categories. Grouped bars make the category-level profit gap immediately visible (Furniture's thin margin vs Technology's strong one).

In [3]:
cat_summary = df.groupby("Category")[["Sales", "Profit"]].sum().sort_values("Sales", ascending=False)

fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(8, 5))
x = np.arange(len(cat_summary))
width = 0.35

bars1 = ax.bar(x - width/2, cat_summary["Sales"], width, label="Total Sales", color=PALETTE[1])
bars2 = ax.bar(x + width/2, cat_summary["Profit"], width, label="Total Profit", color=PALETTE[5])

ax.set_title("Total Sales vs Profit by Category (2023–2024)", fontsize=14, fontweight="bold")
ax.set_xlabel("Category")
ax.set_ylabel("Amount (USD)")
ax.set_xticks(x)
ax.set_xticklabels(cat_summary.index)
ax.legend()
ax.bar_label(bars1, fmt="$%.0f", fontsize=8, padding=2)
ax.bar_label(bars2, fmt="$%.0f", fontsize=8, padding=2)
sns.despine()
plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig("outputs/01_bar_sales_profit_by_category.png", bbox_inches="tight")
plt.show()

Insight: Technology drives the highest sales and, proportionally, the highest profit — it's the strongest category per dollar sold. Furniture generates solid sales volume but a much smaller profit share, consistent with typically thin (sometimes near-zero) furniture margins.

3. Line Chart — Monthly Sales Trend¶

Tracks how total monthly sales evolved across the two-year window, split by category, to surface seasonality (e.g. holiday-season spikes).

In [4]:
monthly = df.groupby(["Month", "Category"])["Sales"].sum().reset_index()

fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(11, 5.5))
for cat, color in zip(df["Category"].unique(), PALETTE[::3]):
    sub = monthly[monthly["Category"] == cat]
    ax.plot(sub["Month"], sub["Sales"], marker="o", markersize=4, linewidth=2, label=cat, color=color)

ax.set_title("Monthly Sales Trend by Category (2023–2024)", fontsize=14, fontweight="bold")
ax.set_xlabel("Month")
ax.set_ylabel("Sales (USD)")
ax.legend(title="Category")
ax.tick_params(axis="x", rotation=45)
sns.despine()
plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig("outputs/02_line_monthly_sales_trend.png", bbox_inches="tight")
plt.show()

Insight: All three categories show a visible uptick in November–December each year, consistent with holiday-season buying. Technology's peaks are the sharpest, suggesting it is the most seasonally sensitive category and a good candidate for targeted Q4 promotions.

4. Pie Chart — Sales Share by Region¶

In [5]:
region_sales = df.groupby("Region")["Sales"].sum().sort_values(ascending=False)

fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(7, 7))
colors = sns.color_palette("viridis", len(region_sales))
wedges, texts, autotexts = ax.pie(
    region_sales,
    labels=region_sales.index,
    autopct="%1.1f%%",
    startangle=90,
    colors=colors,
    explode=[0.05 if v == region_sales.max() else 0 for v in region_sales],
    wedgeprops={"edgecolor": "white", "linewidth": 1.5},
)
plt.setp(autotexts, size=10, weight="bold", color="white")
ax.set_title("Share of Total Sales by Region", fontsize=14, fontweight="bold")
plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig("outputs/03_pie_sales_by_region.png", bbox_inches="tight")
plt.show()

Insight: Sales are fairly evenly split across the four regions, with the leading region only a few points ahead of the rest — there's no single dominant market, so regional strategy should focus on lifting the weakest region rather than over-indexing on the top one.

6. Scatter Plot — Sales vs Profit by Category¶

Each point is one order; color encodes category and size encodes quantity, to see whether higher-value orders reliably convert into higher profit.

In [8]:
fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(9, 6.5))
sample = df.sample(min(800, len(df)), random_state=1)  # sample for readability

sns.scatterplot(
    data=sample, x="Sales", y="Profit", hue="Category", size="Quantity",
    sizes=(20, 200), alpha=0.65, palette="viridis", ax=ax
)
ax.axhline(0, color="crimson", linestyle="--", linewidth=1, alpha=0.7)
ax.set_title("Sales vs Profit by Category (bubble size = Quantity)", fontsize=14, fontweight="bold")
ax.set_xlabel("Sales (USD)")
ax.set_ylabel("Profit (USD)")
ax.legend(bbox_to_anchor=(1.02, 1), loc="upper left", borderaxespad=0)
sns.despine()
plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig("outputs/05_scatter_sales_vs_profit.png", bbox_inches="tight")
plt.show()

Insight: Technology orders (upper band) scale profit with sales fairly consistently. Furniture orders cluster closer to the zero line and dip below it more often even at high sales values — confirming that high revenue alone doesn't guarantee high profit in that category.

7. Bonus — Interactive Dashboard (Plotly)¶

Power BI / Tableau aren't available in this environment, so this section builds an equivalent interactive HTML dashboard with Plotly: hover tooltips, zoom, and filterable legends, exported as a standalone file you can open in any browser (no software needed) or embed elsewhere.

In [9]:
fig = make_subplots(
    rows=2, cols=2,
    subplot_titles=("Monthly Sales Trend", "Sales by Category", "Profit Distribution", "Region Sales Share"),
    specs=[[{"type": "xy"}, {"type": "xy"}], [{"type": "xy"}, {"type": "domain"}]],
)

monthly_total = df.groupby("Month")["Sales"].sum().reset_index()
fig.add_trace(go.Scatter(x=monthly_total["Month"], y=monthly_total["Sales"],
                          mode="lines+markers", name="Sales", line=dict(color="#440154")), row=1, col=1)

cat_sales = df.groupby("Category")["Sales"].sum().reset_index()
fig.add_trace(go.Bar(x=cat_sales["Category"], y=cat_sales["Sales"], name="Sales by Category",
                      marker_color=["#440154", "#31688e", "#35b779"]), row=1, col=2)

fig.add_trace(go.Histogram(x=df["Profit"], nbinsx=40, name="Profit", marker_color="#31688e"), row=2, col=1)

reg_sales = df.groupby("Region")["Sales"].sum().reset_index()
fig.add_trace(go.Pie(labels=reg_sales["Region"], values=reg_sales["Sales"], name="Region"), row=2, col=2)

fig.update_layout(
    height=800, width=1100, showlegend=False,
    title_text="Retail Sales Dashboard (2023–2024)", title_font_size=20,
    template="plotly_white",
)
fig.write_html("outputs/06_interactive_dashboard.html")
fig.show()

Summary of Key Insights¶

  1. Technology is the strongest category on both sales and profit; Furniture sells well but barely profits.
  2. Sales for every category spike in November–December, pointing to strong holiday seasonality.
  3. Regional sales are evenly distributed — no single region dominates the business.
  4. A meaningful share of orders are sold at a loss, concentrated in heavily discounted, low-margin orders.
  5. Discounting on Furniture is the single biggest lever for improving overall profitability.
